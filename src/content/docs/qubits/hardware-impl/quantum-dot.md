title: "Quantum Dot (Spin) Qubits"
---
### How They Work
Quantum dots are super small semiconductor crystals (2-10 nanometers) that create potential wells capable of confining electrons in all three dimensions. This confinement creates discretized energy levels - the smaller the confinement, the greater the distance between energy levels. By using the two lowest energy levels as $\ket{0}$ and $\ket{1}$, we can control the qubit by manipulating electron spin for spin qubits, or moving electrons in and out for charge qubits. In this chapter, we will focus mainly on spin qubits.

Spin qubits are initialized by confining electrons in quantum dots with silicon or gallium arsenide. We apply gates by manipulating the spin using oscillating magnetic fields (electron spin resonance) and measure state by converting spin information to charge, then calibrating current. Electrons trapped in the potential wells mentioned earlier can then be controlled in regards to their electron number, energy levels, and coupling between dots.

### Pros
Due to their small size, spin qubits are incredibly scalable and tunable. They are semiconductor compatible and easy to fabricate, thanks to years of advancements in silicon processing technology. In addition, quantum dots have long coherence times, and some can operate at slightly higher temperatures than the absolute-zero necessary for several other solid-state qubits.

### Cons
Quantum dots are still in a relatively early stage and will require more development for large-scale usage. More importantly, they are complex to manipulate, requiring not only super precise voltage control but also isotopically purified Si-28. Overall, this qubit technology depends heavily on fabrication fidelity.
