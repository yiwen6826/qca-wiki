title: Topological Qubits
---
### How They Work
Until recently, topological qubits were purely theoretical. Microsoft’s Majorana 1 chip, released in February 2025, claimed to be powered by the world’s first topological qubits, although it was received with some skepticism among the scientific community 👀
Regardless, conceptually, topological qubits are patterns of paths of anyons (a type of quasi-particle). These anyons are rearranged into braids using gates, and measurement consists of unbraiding these anyons to determine their configuration. 

### Pros
One of the main pros of topological qubits is their intrinsic error correction. Rather than a single fragile state, quantum info is distributed across multiple particles and their interactions, leading to nonlocal encoding that protects the qubits from environmental disturbances. Also, the stability of topological qubits makes them suitable for large-scale manufacturing and future commercialization.

### Cons
Today, topological qubits remain largely theoretical. It is extremely difficult to braid quasiparticles with sufficient accuracy to operate quantum computers and significant research is still being done in this area.
